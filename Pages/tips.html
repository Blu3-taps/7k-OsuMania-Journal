Osu!Mania 7k Tips & Advice

-Getting started on 7k-

1. Lane Recognition

In 4k+ keymodes, lane recognition is cruicial.

This is the most basic of basic, you need to master this first.

To start this off, you should play songs that are 1★ and below.

You don't have to hit accurately yet, just focus on hitting the notes on the right lane.

It'll probably take you about 1-2 weeks for you to start getting used to it.

2. Finger Control

Finger control is very important for 7k!

Unlike 4k, 7k uses your less dominant fingers like your Ring Fingers & Thumb as well.

Those fingers are naturally weaker and awkward to use so you have to train them.

Make it a habit to do finger drills before you play, you can do them in your free time too.

You won't make it far if you don't train your finger control.

3. Consistency

Consistent practice is important to improve, this is key if you want to get better

You should practice daily but take rests when needed, do NOT overdo it.

Consistency does not mean "Do it everyday regardless of your status".

It means "Do it when you should and rest when you need".

It's entirely up to you how you'll do it.

4. Good Habits

While You are getting started on 7k, good habits should be built here.

Healthy habits help to ensure that you can progress smoothly

Things like tapping lightly, developing good accuracy etc.

Fix your bad habits while you're still early so they don't slow you down,

They'll be frustrating to fix once they've developed

-How to improve in 7k-

1. Limit Breaking

Breaking your limits and reaching higher difficulties is part of the climb.

To break your limits, you should play songs that are slightly higher than your comfort range.

BUT! Only slightly more difficult, you should struggle but still be able to play it.

You won't improve if it's too hard for you, if you get <90% you should kick it down a notch.

➜ I'd suggest about 0.1★~0.3★ higher than your limit

For example, if your limit is 4.3★ you should try songs around 4.4★~4.6★.

2. Pattern Mastery

There are many different types of patterns in 7k.

There'll be patterns you're weak at and patterns you are strong at, it's different for everyone.

You should build on your strengths, but don't neglect your weaknesses

Play different songs to expose yourself to different types of patterns, don't be picky.

If you're having difficulty with certain patterns, it's best to do focused training

Play more songs with the pattern type that you're struggling with

I'd personally suggest downloading dedicated Training Packs and playing them

3. Accuracy

Aside from just being able to hit patterns, you need to hit them on time as well.

This is called Accuracy,

➜ How well you hit a note relative to its timing

There are 3 main aspects in Accuracy

  • Main Accuracy
  • Marvellous to Perfect Ratio
  • Unstable Rate (UR) & Error

1. Main Accuracy

This is the Overall Accuracy displayed in --.--% (e.g. 96.73%)

This Accuracy is determined by the Judgements Types scored during the song

There are 6 different Judgement Types (From best to worst) ⮟

  • Marvellous
  • Perfect
  • Great
  • Good
  • Bad
  • Miss

You should aim to get less inaccuracies over time as you set scores

Focus on getting Marvellous & Perfects, less or none of the others.

Aim for >99% on a song before moving on from it (You can still go for 100% after)

2. Marvellous to Perfect Ratio

This is the proportion of your Marvellous hits to your Perfect hits

It represents how precisely and consistently you hit notes

For some context, a Perfect is just a slightly off-timed Marvellous

Ratio is Marvellous ÷ Perfect, but what does it mean?

e.g. 2500 marv ÷ 250 perf = 10

This means you get 1 Perfect for every 10 Marvellous

You'd want to aim to get more Marvellous per Perfect

3. Unstable Rate (UR) & Error

This is the consistency & timing of your hits

It tells you overall how well you hit the notes on time

What is Error?

When we hit notes we have a timing error of ±_ms (milliseconds)

It's displayed like "Error: -ms - +ms avg"

-ms is how early you hit on average ⇄ +ms is how late you hit on average

Aim to get smaller hit errors

What is Unstable Rate?

Aim to cut down on inaccuracies like greats, goods, bads and misses

After that, focus on your [ Marvellous : Perfect ] ratio.

The higher the ratio the better, try to get more Marvellous and less Perfects

You can use the HR (HardRock) Mod to improve your accuracy by making timings tighter.

If you want to play around with how Judgements affect accuracy, go to Calculations

4. Speed

Are you struggling to hit some patterns because they're too fast?

Different patterns follow different rhythms, some slow and others fast

You can improve your speed naturally by playing faster patterns like Burst Streams/Rolls

Learning to relax and tap lightly is also necessary for speed, tensing up is a no-go.

You can also become faster by playing the DT (Double Time) Mod on lower difficulties

Though, DT makes a song significantly harder so I'd recommend:

➜ Only play DT if you have >99% & an FC (Full Combo) on the song no mod first.

Another way is to play "rate" songs

However, there aren't a lot of "rate" songs and they're higher in difficulty

So they aren't very accessible for beginners to practice.

"rate" songs have difficulties that look like this ⮟

  • Difficulty name
  • Difficulty name [1.1x rate]
  • Difficulty name [1.2x rate]
  • Difficulty name [1.3x rate]
  • Difficulty name [1.4x rate]

"rate" songs have difficulties with steps of incremental speed

Usually in steps of ±0.05x or ±0.1x speed, they can be 0.8x or 0.9x speed as well

These songs are meant to help you get faster progressively

You should move to the next rate only when you can get at least 98% on the previous

5. Stamina

Do you find yourself unable to keep up in a song?

Maybe you find patterns starting to look a blur?

Maybe your hands start to ache and they become hard to move?

This is called Stamina,

➜ the ability to endure prolonged effort without tiring out

There are 2 types of stamina⮟

Mental Stamina and Physical Stamina

1. Mental Stamina

This is also known as Reading Stamina,

➜ The ability to read & process patterns continuously without fail

Reading Stamina is one of the bigger hurdles early on as a beginner

For 7k especially, there are more lanes and notes to keep track of than in 4k

Making it harder to maintain focus and you'll break once you can't keep up.

So how do you build Reading Stamina?

Pretty simple actually.

Just practice and play more, like seriously. (Sounds generic but I'm not joking.)

The more you expose yourself to new patterns, the more you adapt.

Ocassionally play songs that push your limits, you should feel like you're seeing stars

That's your brain adapting, let it.

2. Physical Stamina

This is your Tapping Stamina,

➜ The ability to tap & hit patterns continuously without tiring out

Tapping Stamina can be affected by ⮟

  • Song Duration
  • Pattern Density
  • Tapping Technique

Songs with a long length forces you to endure through many sections
